What to eat

France is famous for its culinary scene, but the food is as diverse as the country itself. Traditions date back to the middle ages, but were not recorded until the 1600s when the first cookbooks came out. As they colonized other countries, French food spread around the world and influenced other cultures.

Looking at a French menu for the first time can be intimidating. Below is some information to  ensure you order the best possible meal.

Haute Cuisine

Directly translated to “high cuisine,” this term refers to a shift in French cooking. Chefs moved from cooking extravagant and large meals to focusing on quality and moderation. Chefs turned their attention to ingredients and techniques, which improved dishes.

Nouvelle Cuisine

This style of cooking emerged in the 1900s, which focused on simplicity and ingredients. Dishes cooked in this style are presented simply but beautifully, and a strong emphasis on quality.
